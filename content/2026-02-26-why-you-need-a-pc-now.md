*[B-roll: Factory footage, shipping containers, news headlines about chip supply and tariffs]*

"The AI boom isn't just happening in hyperscale data centers. Every major tech company on earth is scrambling to buy GPUs. NVIDIA, AMD — their best chips go to enterprise customers first. What's left filters down to the consumer market, and with less supply chasing more demand, prices go up."

*[SHOW: Simple supply/demand diagram graphic]*

**POINT 2 — DRAM Cycle is Tightening:**
"DRAM — which is the memory in your RAM sticks and your VRAM — runs in manufacturing cycles. Right now, the industry is entering an UPSWING phase. Prices are rising, manufacturers are slowing production to maintain margins, and that cost gets passed directly to you."

*[PRO TIP BOX: "Historical pattern: When DRAM enters an upcycle, prices can rise 50-100% over 18-24 months."]*

**POINT 3 — Geopolitical and Trade Pressures:**
"Tariffs, trade restrictions, and supply chain reshoring efforts are adding cost to every chip that comes out of Asia. These aren't temporary. They're structural."

"Bottom line: if you're waiting for prices to 'go back to normal' — there may not be a normal to go back to. This is the new floor."
